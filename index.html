A Tale of Artificial Intelligence

My name is Hassam Ullah Sheikh. I am currently a 2nd year Ph.D at University of Central Florida under the supervision Dr Lotzi Bölöni. Before coming to UCF, I was a Masters student at University of Manchester where I was advised by Dr Ke Chen.
My current research revolves around applying reinforcement learning in simulated environments to tackle real world problems. I am a reinforcement learning enthusiast and I personally believe that reinforcement learning is the key for artificial general intelligence.

Recent Work

Maximal Physical Protection for a VIP using Robot Bodyguards

Currently working on creating reinforcement learning based algorithms for robotic bodyguards in Unreal Engine to provide secure pathway for a VIP to navigate in crowded environment.
Tools: Unreal Engine, YARL, Keras

YARL: Yet Another Reinforcement Learning Package

YARL is a generalized keras based reinforcement learning package that I am currently building to faciliate rapid reinforcement learning development. As of now algorithms that are implmented in YARL are DQN, Double Q learning and DDPG.
Tools: Keras

Who is Speaking? Male or Female

Proposed a new classification method for speech based gender identification systems using a combination of Shifted Delta Coefficients with Pitch and trained using Gaussian Mixture Models. This method gave 81% accuracy on Youtube videos. Implmented Recurrent Convolution Neural Network to improve the performance of the system to 93%.
Tools: Keras, MATLAB
